1. Executive Summary

The Problem

NorthSouth Coffee's loyalty programme is in crisis. Monthly active users have fallen 40% in six months (from 80,000 to 48,000), reward redemption sits at 12% against an industry average of 35%, and the programme's Net Promoter Score of -12 means customers are actively warning others away. The root cause: customers sign up, redeem a free welcome drink, and then encounter a "Points Desert" — a barren stretch of low-value interactions with no short-term rewards, no personalisation, and no reason to return.

This report synthesises the work of a four-agent pipeline that has diagnosed the problem, designed the solution, built the technical implementation, and created the launch strategy. The result is "NorthSouth Roast Rewards" — a comprehensive programme redesign that replaces the Points Desert with:

  • A Milestone Ladder delivering rewards at 3, 5, 8, 12, and 20 visits
  • Smart Lifecycle Messaging that sends the right message at the right time
  • A "Second Cup" Onboarding Journey with Triple Points to drive second visits within 14 days
  • Gamification: Weekly Challenges, Visit Streaks with Streak Shields, and random Surprise & Delight Drops
  • App Utility: personalised home screen, store finder, and digital menu

Contradictions Resolved

  1. Budget allocation: The Designer allocated the full €250,000 to technology phases. The Communicator allocated €68,000 to communications. Resolution: the €250,000 was always intended as total programme budget. The Coder's infrastructure (€182,000 for development + €1,200/month) plus the Communicator's €68,000 fits within the total envelope.
  2. Deployment timeline vs. communication timeline: The Coder's Phase 1 starts in Week 1, but the Communicator's pre-launch communications start in Week 5. Resolution: this is correct — you build before you announce.
  3. Subscription model: The Researcher noted Pret's subscription success; the Manager explicitly excluded subscriptions from scope. Resolution: correct decision. The Milestone Ladder achieves similar frequency-driving effects through gamification rather than financial commitment.

7. Recommendations to the Board

Recommendation 1: Approve and Begin Immediately

What: Approve the full €250,000 programme budget and authorise the 8-week build starting within 5 business days.

Why: Every week of delay costs approximately €66,000 in lost incremental revenue. The programme's 40% MAU decline is accelerating — at current trajectory, MAU could fall below 30,000 within 3-4 months.

Recommendation 2: Commission GDPR Consent Audit Immediately

What: Direct the Data Protection Officer to audit marketing consent records for all 310,000 registered members within 10 business days.

Why: The re-engagement campaign targeting 100,000+ lapsed users is the highest-impact single initiative. If consent records are invalid, a re-permission campaign must run first, adding 2 weeks.

Recommendation 3: Hire/Contract Two Additional Engineers

What: Bring on two contract backend engineers for the 8-week build. Budget: approximately €40,000-50,000 from the technology allocation.

Why: The technical scope (15+ tables, 25+ endpoints, 7 services) is ambitious for 8 weeks. Additional engineers provide redundancy and reduce single-point-of-failure risk.

Recommendation 4: Set Up Real-Time KPI Dashboard in Week 1

What: Prioritise the analytics dashboard as a Week 1 deliverable (basic version), not Phase 3.

Why: "You can't improve what you can't measure" is especially true for a programme recovering from a 40% decline.

Recommendation 5: Plan for Continuous Iteration

What: Allocate €5,000-8,000/month post-launch for ongoing optimisation: new challenges, seasonal campaigns, A/B testing, and feature refinement.

Why: The risk of launching and then neglecting the programme is precisely what caused the original failure.
